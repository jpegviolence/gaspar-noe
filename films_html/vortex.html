VORTEX

The last days of an elderly couple stricken by dementia

"Vortex" tells the story of an elderly couple, played by Dario Argento and Françoise Lebrun, who are facing the end of their lives. The film is a slow and meditative exploration of the bonds that hold us together and the inevitability of death.

Noé uses his signature style of long takes and dynamic camera work to create a sense of intimacy and closeness between the characters. The camera often lingers on their faces, capturing the wrinkles and imperfections of old age, and highlighting the deep connection between the couple. Despite the film's melancholy subject matter, there is a sense of hopefulness and beauty that pervades the film. Noé often uses shots of nature, such as birds in flight or waves crashing on the shore, to evoke a sense of timelessness and continuity. One of the most impressive aspects of "Vortex" is the performances by Argento and Lebrun. Both actors bring a profound depth of emotion to their roles, and their chemistry on screen is palpable. Their conversations and interactions capture the essence of a long-term relationship, with all its ups and downs, joys and sorrows.

As with many of Gaspar Noé's films, "Vortex" is not for everyone. The film is slow-paced, and its subject matter may be difficult to watch for some viewers. However, for those willing to engage with its themes and characters, "Vortex" is a deeply moving and profound work of cinema. Overall, "Vortex" serves as a reminder of the power of cinema to explore the deepest, most meaningful aspects of the human experience. It is a beautiful and haunting meditation on love, life, and the passage of time, and a testament to Noé's continued relevance in the world of film.
